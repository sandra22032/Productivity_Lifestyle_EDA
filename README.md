# Productivity & Lifestyle Habits – Exploratory Data Analysis

## Project Overview

This project explores how different lifestyle habits are related to self-reported productivity among young adults. The analysis focuses on habits such as sleep, exercise, screen time, planning, and phone usage to identify patterns and trends using Python.

## Objectives

- Clean and prepare the dataset for analysis
- Explore relationships between lifestyle habits and productivity
- Create visualizations to identify patterns
- Summarize the key findings from the data

## Dataset

The dataset was collected through a survey and contains information on participants' lifestyle habits, daily routines, and self-reported productivity levels.

## Tools & Libraries Used

- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

## Key Findings

- People who exercised every day reported the highest productivity.
- Higher screen time was generally linked to lower productivity.
- People who planned their day regularly reported better productivity.
- Participants who checked their phones first thing in the morning tended to have lower productivity.
- Students mostly struggled with procrastination, while working professionals reported stress more often.
- The amount of time spent on social media seemed to matter more than the platform being used.
- People who used planners or calendars reported higher productivity than those who did not use any productivity tools.

## Future Improvements

- Collect responses from a larger group of people
- Build an interactive dashboard using Power BI or Tableau
- Perform statistical analysis to validate the findings
